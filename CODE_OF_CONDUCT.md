# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

The Agora Framework is an institutional open source project committed to fostering an environment of rigorous intellectual exchange, mutual respect, and inclusive participation. We, as contributors, maintainers, and stewards of this project, pledge to make participation in the Agora community a harassment-free experience for everyone — regardless of age, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We further pledge to engage with the substantive work of this project — open source governance, digital sovereignty, institutional policy — with the intellectual honesty, professional integrity, and civic commitment that such work demands.

---

## Our Standards

### Expected Behaviour

The following behaviours are expected of all community members:

- **Professional Discourse**: Engage with ideas and arguments, not individuals. Critique proposals on their merits.
- **Intellectual Rigour**: Ground contributions in evidence, reference authoritative sources, and acknowledge the limits of your expertise.
- **Inclusive Participation**: Actively welcome and accommodate participants from diverse institutional, cultural, and technical backgrounds.
- **Constructive Criticism**: Offer feedback that is specific, actionable, and respectful. Accept feedback with the same standard.
- **Transparency**: Declare relevant affiliations, conflicts of interest, or institutional positions when they may bear on contributions.
- **Attribution and Respect for Intellectual Property**: Credit sources, respect licence conditions, and honour the work of others — including the projects upon which this framework builds.
- **Institutional Good Faith**: Recognise that this project serves the public interest. Contributions must not be instrumentalised for private or partisan advantage.

### Unacceptable Behaviour

The following behaviours are considered unacceptable and will not be tolerated:

- Harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks in any form
- Discriminatory language or conduct based on any protected characteristic
- Deliberate misrepresentation of others' positions or work
- Repeated and disruptive off-topic contributions
- Unauthorised disclosure of private information
- Bad-faith contributions intended to introduce inaccuracies, weaken governance standards, or undermine the framework's public-interest mission
- Any conduct that would be considered unprofessional or inappropriate in a European institutional or academic setting

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all Agora Framework community spaces, including:

- The GitHub repository (issues, pull requests, discussions, code review comments)
- Mailing lists and email communications related to the project
- Official events, workshops, or presentations where community members represent the Agora Framework
- Direct private communications where a community member is representing the project

## Enforcement

### Reporting

Instances of unacceptable behaviour may be reported to the Agora Framework maintainers at:

**conduct@iaauteco.org**

All reports will be handled with confidentiality. The reporting process is as follows:

1. Submit a report by email with a description of the incident, the parties involved, and any available evidence.
2. A maintainer not party to the incident will acknowledge receipt within 72 hours.
3. An initial assessment will be completed within 14 days.
4. The reporter will be informed of the outcome, subject to confidentiality constraints.

### Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these graduated response guidelines:

#### 1. Correction
**Trigger:** Minor violation, first instance, assumed good faith.  
**Response:** Private, written communication identifying the specific behaviour and explaining why it is inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

#### 2. Warning
**Trigger:** Repeated minor violations or a single more serious violation.  
**Response:** Formal written warning with a defined period of restricted engagement. Continuation of the behaviour will result in a temporary ban.

#### 3. Temporary Ban
**Trigger:** Serious violation of community standards or sustained inappropriate behaviour.  
**Response:** Temporary suspension of all interaction rights within the project spaces. Duration will be specified in writing.

#### 4. Permanent Ban
**Trigger:** Pattern of violations, severe misconduct, demonstrated bad faith, or harm to the project's integrity.  
**Response:** Permanent exclusion from all project spaces and interactions.
