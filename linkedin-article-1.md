# Introducing the Agora Framework: A Reference Architecture for Open Source Program Offices

*Published by IAAUTECO Inc. | Open Source Systems & Sovereign AI*

---

Today, IAAUTECO Inc. is releasing **Agora** — an open source reference framework for establishing and operating Open Source Program Offices (OSPOs) in public administrations, European institutions, research organisations, and mission-driven enterprises.

The name is deliberate. In the ancient Greek city-state, the *agora* was not merely a marketplace — it was the institutional heart of civic life: the space where citizens gathered to deliberate, legislate, and govern their commons collectively. An OSPO plays precisely this role within a modern organisation. It is the forum through which an institution's relationship with the open source ecosystem is governed openly, accountably, and in the public interest.

## Why This, Why Now

The strategic centrality of open source software is beyond reasonable debate. More than 95% of commercial and institutional software incorporates open source components. European public bodies operate critical services on open source infrastructure. The EU Open Source Strategy, the Interoperable Europe Act, and the NIS2 Directive all impose governance obligations that presuppose a level of institutional open source maturity that most organisations do not yet possess.

The governance gap between the operational reality and the institutional capability is real, and its consequences are serious:

- **Legal exposure** through untracked licence obligations
- **Security vulnerability** through unmonitored dependencies — directly in scope of NIS2 and the EU Cyber Resilience Act
- **Strategic dependency** on ecosystems in which the organisation has no governance voice
- **Sovereignty erosion** through governance structures that defer to non-European platforms and standards

The OSPO is the institutional response to this challenge. The Agora Framework is the architecture through which that response can be deployed with rigour, interoperability, and accountability.

## What Agora Provides

The Agora Framework is structured around **six pillars**:

**I. Institutional Mandate** — the formal authority, governance structure, and resource allocation required for an effective OSPO

**II. Policy Architecture** — a six-domain policy framework covering consumption, contribution, publication, security, inner source, and AI/model governance

**III. Compliance and Licence Stewardship** — processes aligned with OpenChain ISO/IEC 5230 and the REUSE Specification for systematic licence compliance

**IV. Contribution Governance** — structured workflows for inbound, outbound, and inner source contributions

**V. Community and Ecosystem Engagement** — mechanisms for upstream governance participation and ecosystem investment

**VI. Metrics and Accountability** — CHAOSS-aligned metrics and reporting, culminating in an annual Open Source Programme Report

The framework includes a full **white paper**, an **institutional OSPO Policy Template** (immediately adaptable), and complete governance documentation — all available in Markdown, OOXML (Word), and ODF (LibreOffice) format to maximise institutional accessibility.

## Built on Open Standards

Agora does not reinvent the wheel. It is explicitly aligned with the standards and frameworks that the global open source governance community has developed over the past decade:

- **OpenChain ISO/IEC 5230** for licence compliance programmes
- **SPDX ISO/IEC 5962** for Software Bills of Materials
- **REUSE Specification** for per-file copyright metadata
- **CHAOSS** for community health metrics
- **publiccode.yml** for machine-readable public software metadata
- The **European Interoperability Framework** and relevant EU legislation

The framework itself is released under the **European Union Public Licence v1.2 (EUPL-1.2)** — the licence designed specifically for European public software, compatible with the GPL family, and aligned with European judicial jurisdiction.

## An Institutional Act, An Open Invitation

Releasing Agora as an open source project is itself a governance act. The framework is governed by the same instruments it recommends: a published governance model, a code of conduct, structured contribution guidelines, and a transparent decision-making process.

This is not a commercial product. It is a public infrastructure contribution — an institutional investment in the shared governance commons of European open source.

We invite institutions, practitioners, legal professionals, policy experts, and technical leaders to engage with the framework: to adopt it, adapt it, critique it, and contribute to it.
